An **SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle)** is a **legally separate entity** created for a **specific, narrow purpose**, commonly to **isolate risk**, **hold assets**, and/or **raise financing**.
![[spv-simple.png]]

# Why SPVs Exist (Core Ideas)

## 1.1 Risk isolation (“bankruptcy remoteness”)
- The SPV is **separate** from the sponsor (the company that sets it up).
- If the sponsor gets into trouble, the SPV’s assets are **intended** to be protected from the sponsor’s creditors.

## 1.2 Structured financing
- The SPV can **issue securities** (notes, bonds, certificates) to investors.
- Those securities are typically repaid using **cash flows from the SPV’s assets**.

## 1.3 Accounting / regulatory / operational reasons
- Can help with **financing terms**, **capital requirements**, **asset management**, and **ring-fencing** project risks.
- (Note: the legitimacy depends on transparency and compliance. SPVs are not “automatically shady.”)
