# Welfare Economics
- Consumer surplus measures economic welfare from the buyer’s side
    - Willingness to pay is the maximum amount that a buyer will pay for a good
    - It measures how much the buyer values the good or service
    - Consumer surplus is the buyer’s willingness to pay for a good - the amount the buyer actually pays for it, which is the area below the demand curve and above the price
- Producer surplus measures economic welfare from the seller’s side
    - Producer surplus is the amount a seller is paid for a good - the seller’s cost
    - It measures the benefit to sellers participating in a market and equals the area below the price and above the supply curve
    
![[Screenshot 2026-01-01 at 23.26.29.png]]
## Market Efficiency
$$
\begin{align*}
\text{Total surplus} &= \text{Consumer surplus} + \text{Producer surplus} \\
&= \text{Value to buyers} - \text{Cost to seller}
\end{align*}
$$
![[total-surplus.png | 400]]
- **==Efficiency==** is the property of a resource allocation of maximizing the total surplus received by all members of society
- An allocation is **==Pareto efficient==** if no individual can be made better off without another being made worse off
    - 不能在不让别人变差的情况下，让任何一个人变得更好
- In the equilibrium of a competitive market:
    - the sum of consumer surplus and producer surplus is maximized
    - consumer surplus cannot be raised without lowering producer surplus

> [!tip] In a competitive market with no externalities, no market power, and complete information, the market equilibrium is Pareto efficient.
## Quantity Restriction

![[quantity-restriction-welfare.png| 400]]
- 政策如果直接固定的是“数量” → 它不是 shift S/D，而是加一个“数量约束”。
- Supply 曲线本身代表“在每一个价格下，厂商愿意/能够供给多少”——也就是供给行为的规则没变（技术、成本、税、补贴、投入价格等都没变），所以曲线不需要移动。这里变化的是：即使在某个价格下厂商“愿意供给更多”，也被限制不能供给超过 Quantity’。这叫数量管制/生产限制（quantity restriction）：改变的是“允许交易的数量”，不是“供给规律”。
# Comparative & Absolute Advantage
- ==Absolute Advantage==: The comparison among producers of a good according to their productivity - absolute advantage
    - The producer that requires a smaller quantity of inputs to produce a good is said to have an absolute advantage in producing that good
- ==Comparative Advantage==: The producer who has the smaller opportunity cost of producing a good is said to have a comparative advantage in producing that good
    - ==Benefits of Trade==: Trade can benefit everyone in a society because it allows people to specialize in activities in which they have a comparative advantage
# International Trade

> [!warning] A primary reason why nations conduct international trade is because: 
> Resources are not equally distributed to all trading nations.

- If domestic price < world price → country has a comparative advantage → ***exporter***
- If domestic price > world price → country is an ***importer***

## Restrictions on Trade
### Deadweight Loss ^deadweight-loss

Deadweight loss（无谓损失）= 因为政策或扭曲，原本对买卖双方都有好处的交易没发生，导致社会总福利“白白少掉”的那一块。
### Tariff
With a tariff, total surplus in the market decreases by an amount referred to as a [[#^deadweight-loss|deadweight loss]]

![[Screenshot 2026-01-02 at 17.21.38.png | 450]]

|                        | Before Tariff               | After Tariff        | Change              |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------- | ------------------- |
| **Consumer Surplus**   | $A + B + C + D + E + F$     | $A + B$             | $− (C + D + E + F)$ |
| **Producer Surplus**   | $G$                         | $C + G$             | $+ C$               |
| **Government Revenue** | None                        | $E$                 | $+ E$               |
| **Total Surplus**      | $A + B + C + D + E + F + G$ | $A + B + C + E + G$ | $− (D + F)$         |
Note: The areas $D + F$ represent the [[#^deadweight-loss|deadweight loss]] of the tariff — the part of total surplus that disappears rather than being transferred to producers or the government.
### Import Quota
An import quota is a limit on the quantity of a good that can be produced abroad and sold domestically

![[Screenshot 2026-01-02 at 02.50.44.png | 450]]
- Isolandian price with quota 会高于 world price，不是因为“进口已经用完”，而是因为“市场预期进口是被限制的”，所以价格必须上升到一个水平。
- $D$ and $F$ are deadweight loss

### Non-Tariff Barriers（非关税壁垒）
Non-tariff barriers（NTBs）= 不通过关税，但仍然限制进口的手段。
- Complex or discriminatory rules of origin and quality conditions
    - Technical and health and safety issues
- [[sanitary]] and [[phytosanitary]] measures
    - Measures to protect humans, animals, and plants from diseases, pests, or contaminants, like food safety standards 
- Administrative regulations
    - [[Red tape]] 
- Currency manipulation
### The [[argument|Arguments]] for Restricting Trade（为什么要限制贸易？）
- Climate change mitigation 
- Food security
- Geopolitical concerns (like Unfair Competition argument) 
- National Security argument 
- Public health concerns 
- Resilience 
    - The ability of an economic system to continue operating and quickly recover when encountering shocks. 经济系统在遭遇冲击时，仍然能持续运转、并快速恢复的能力。
- Strategic competitiveness ([[Infant Industry]] argument) 
    - 新兴产业一开始成本高，需要保护，等规模经济形成
- Other (like Jobs argument, or Protection as a Bargaining Chip)
