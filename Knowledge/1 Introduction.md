# 1 Thinking like an Economist

## 1.1 Inductive reasoning vs. Deductive Reasoning
[[Inductive vs. Deductive reasoning]]
- **Inductive reasoning**: From observations → find patterns → propose hypothesis → maybe form a theory.
- **Deductive Reasoning**: Start from a theory → make a hypothesis → test with observations → confirm or reject.
## 1.2 Positive vs. Normative analysis
- Positive statements are statements that attempt to describe the world as it is (“descriptive analysis”). For instance:o
    - “An increase in the minimum wage will cause a decrease in employment among the least skilled.”
    - “Higher federal budget deficits will cause interest rates to increase.”
- Normative statements are statements about how the world should be (“prescriptive analysis”) For instance:
    - “The income gains from a higher minimum wage **are worth more than** any slight reductions in employment.”
    - “Governments **should be** allowed to collect from tobacco companies the costs of treating smoking-related illnesses among the poor.”

Economists may disagree about the **validity** of alternative positive theories or they may have **different values and therefore different normative views** about what policy should try to accomplish.
## 1.3 THE ECONOMIST AS POLICY ADVISOR
- When economists are trying to explain the world, they are **scientists**
- When economists are trying to change the world, they are **policy advisors**

# 2 Economic Models
Models are used to simplify reality in order to improve our understanding of the world.
A model will contain a number of variables:
[[Endogenous vs. Exogenous Variable]]
- **Endogenous variables**: A variable whose value is determined within the model.
- **Exogenous variables**: A variable whose value is determined outside the model.
- **Cause and effect**: exogenous variables determine endogenous variables
- **Ceteris paribus**: When we say “ceteris paribus,” we mean we are only changing one variable, and we assume all other factors remain constant so we can see the pure effect. [[Ceteris paribus]]
## 2.1 Circular-Flow Diagram
The circular flow diagram shows how money flows through markets among households and firms:
![[Circular-Flow-Diagram.png]]
- Factors of Production
    [[Factors of Production]]
    - Inputs used to produce goods and services
    - Land, labour, and capital
- Markets for Factors of Production
    - Households sell / Firms buy
- Markets for Goods and Services
    - Firms sell / Households buy
- Households = 经济体系中的消费者 + 生产要素的拥有者
    [[Households]]
    - Buy and consume goods and services
    - Own and sell factors of production
- Firms
    - Produce and sell goods and services
    - Hire and use factors of production

## 2.2 The Production Possibilities Frontier
The production possibilities frontier shows the combinations of output that the economy can possibly produce given the available factors of production and the available production technology.

![[Screenshot 2025-12-02 at 21.49.44.png]]

- **Efficiency**
    - Points on the PPF curve
        → Resources are fully utilized, with no waste
        → The economy is operating at its most efficient level
    - Points inside the curve = inefficient
- **Trade-offs**
    由于资源有限，生产更多一种商品 → 必须减少另一种商品。PPF 的斜率体现了这种取舍关系。
- **Opportunity Cost** = 为了增加一种产出而必须放弃的另一种产出数量。
    The slope of the PPF equals the opportunity cost (the steeper the curve, the greater the opportunity cost). **PPF 的斜率 = 机会成本** （曲线越陡，机会成本越大）
- **Economic growth**
    Leading to an outward shift of the Production Possibilities Frontier

# 3 Microeconomics vs. Macroeconomics
- **Microeconomics** is the study of how individual households and firms make decisions and how they interact with one another in markets
- **Macroeconomics** is the study of the economy as a whole. Its goal is to explain the economic changes that affect many households, firms, and markets at once.
    Macroeconomics tries to answer questions like the following:
    - Why is average income high in some countries and low in others?
    - Why do prices rise rapidly in some time periods while they are more stable in others?
    - Why do production and employment expand in some years and contract [[contract]] in others?
