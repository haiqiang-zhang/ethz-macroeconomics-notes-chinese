# Introduce to Phillips Curve
$$
\text{Unemployment Rate}
=
\text{Natural Rate of Unemployment}
-
b(\text{Actual Inflation}-\text{Expected Inflation}) + \text{Supply Shock}
$$
$$u=u_n-b ( \pi - \pi^e )+\varepsilon$$
- $u$：Unemployment rate
- $u_n$​：Natural rate of unemployment
- $\pi$：Actual inflation
- $\pi^e$：Expected inflation
- $\varepsilon$：Supply shock

So Central Bank need to independence

- Why does Expected Inflation Shift the Short-Run Phillips Curve?
     $u=u_n-b ( \pi - \pi^e )+\varepsilon$ -> $\pi = \pi^e + \frac{u_n + \varepsilon - u}{b}$
 
     If expected inflation $\pi^e$ increases by $\Delta$, then for any given unemployment rate $u$ (with $u$ unchanged), $\pi$ will also increase by exactly $\Delta$.

> - Expected Inflation: Shift
> - expansionary/contraction policy: move along

# The Cost of Reducing Inflation
- When the CB slows the rate of money growth, it contracts aggregate demand
- This reduces the quantity of goods and services that firms produce
- This leads to a rise in unemployment

![[Screenshot 2026-01-08 at 21.55.08.png|400]]

> [!NOTE]- Explain the plot
> - Initial state (Point $A$):
>     - Economy is on the long-run Phillips curve
>     - Unemployment = natural rate
>     - Inflation is high
>     - Expected inflation is high
>     - Economy lies on the short-run Phillips curve with high expected inflation (blue)
> - Contractionary monetary policy ($A → B$, short run):
>     - Central bank tightens monetary policy
>     - Aggregate demand falls
>     - Firms reduce output
>     - Unemployment rises
>     - Economy moves **along the same short-run Phillips curve**
>     - Inflation falls, unemployment rises above the natural rate
>       → Short run: *move along* the short-run Phillips curve (no shift)
> - Adjustment of expectations ($B → C$):
>     - Inflation remains lower
>     - High inflation is no longer expected
>     - Wage bargaining becomes more moderate
>     - Firms set lower prices
>     - Expected inflation falls
> - Change in the Phillips curve:
>     - Short-run Phillips curve shifts leftward
>     - Blue curve → red curve
> - Final outcome (Point $C$):
>     - Unemployment returns to the natural rate
>     - Inflation is permanently lower
>     - Economy returns to the long-run Phillips curve
> - Key implication:
>     - Monetary policy affects unemployment only in the short run
>     - Monetary policy affects inflation in the long run

## The Sacrifice Ratio

***The sacrifice ratio*** is the number of percentage points of annual output that is lost in the process of reducing inflation by one percentage point

Sacrifice ratio ≈ 5

## Rational Expectations and the Possibility of Costless Disinflation
- ***Adaptive expectations***: People base their inflation expectations on recently observed inflation. 
    - ⬆️ 上面都是基于这个Adaptive expectations，也就是人们需要时间去调整expectation
- ***Rational expectations***: 
    - People base their inflation expectations on all available information, including information about current and prospective future policies
    - The theory of rational expectations suggests that people optimally use all the information they have, including information about government policies, when forecasting the future
    - 这样就不会有高unemployment的时期了，直接降低inflation rate，而不提高unemployment rate
# New Keynesian Short-Run Aggregate Supply Curve

![[Screenshot 2026-01-08 at 22.55.22.png|450]]
- 经济有闲置产能（$Y$ 远小于潜在产出/充分就业产出 $Y_f$）时：企业要多生产并不难：多用一点机器、加班、招工更容易。  
    - AD 增加主要带来产出增加，价格涨得不多（SRAS 比较“平”）。
- 经济接近满负荷（$Y$ 接近 $Y_f$）时：关键资源开始紧张：熟练工、关键零部件、产线瓶颈、物流、土地/电力容量等。  
    - 企业想再多生产一单位，会越来越“费劲”，成本和加价压力更大。
    - 同样的 AD 增加，更多体现为价格上升，产出增加很小（SRAS 变得很“陡”）。

- Reform tax and welfare policy
- Flexible Labour Markets
- Reducing Government Spending

## Interventionist Supply-Side Policies
Four key aspects to interventionist supply-Side policies:
- Infrastructure Investment
    - Governments involved where there are long term benefits
- Investment in Education and Training
- Research and Development (R&D)
    - Research bodies, tax relief or grants
- Regional or Industrial Policies
    - Designed to encourage investment and the regional multiplier
