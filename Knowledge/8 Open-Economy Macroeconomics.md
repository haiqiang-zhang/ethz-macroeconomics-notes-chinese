# International Flows of Goods and Capital
## Net Capital Outflow (NCO)
- NCO = purchase of foreign assets by domestic residents - purchase of domestic assets by foreigners **= net foreign investment**
> [!example] Example: A Swiss resident buys stock in the Cadbury’s corporation and an American buys stock in Nestlé
> - When a Swiss resident buys stock in a British bank, the purchase raises Swiss net capital outflow (NCO of Swiss ⬆️)
> - When an American resident buys a bond issued by the Swiss government, the purchase reduces Swiss net capital outflow (NCO of Swiss ⬇)


> [!warning] domestic residents购买foreign assets，那么capital（钱）流出，assets流入

### The Equality of Net Exports and Net Capital Outflow
For an economy as a whole, NX and NCO must balance each other so that:
$$NCO = NX$$
- This holds true because every transaction that affects one side must also affect the other side by the same amount
    - 你从国外“买东西”（import）时，钱/资产必须以某种形式流向国外；他国capital流入
    - 你向国外“卖东西”（export）时，你会收到钱/资产（或者获得对方欠你的资产索取权）；本国captial 流出($NCO$)
    - 所以：
        - 货物交易（$NX$）发生多少，
        - 就必然伴随资产交易（$NCO$）发生同样规模，并且**符号相同**
#### Derivation of the Identity

> [!info] Recall [[5 Saving, investment and the financial system#^national-saving]] and [[2 National Accounting#^expenditure-approach-GDP]]

Net exports is a component of GDP:
$$Y = C + I + G + NX$$
National saving is the income of the nation that is left after paying for current consumption and government purchases:
$$Y – C – G = I + NX$$
National saving ($S$) equals $Y – C – G$ so:
$$S = I + NX$$
$$S – I = NX$$
$$S – I = \text{Net Capital Outflow} = NCO = NX$$

## The Balance of Payments (BoP) Accounts
> BoP 是双重记账：跨国“买卖货物/服务”（经常账户，NX）一定伴随“买卖资产”（金融账户，NCO）。所以经常账户和金融账户总能配平。进口导致 NX 下降，同时意味着资本净流入（NCO 下降）。

The BoP consists of: 
- the current account (≈ trade account) 
- the financial (and capital) account
    - Funds flow into Switzerland = credit (+)
        （外国人把钱带进瑞士，通常对应“外国人买瑞士Assets”）
    - Funds flow out of Switzerland = debit (−)
        （瑞士把钱送出去，通常对应“瑞士买外国Assets”）
***Notes: Current account + Financial account (+ Capital account) = 0***

|        | the current account | the financial (and capital) account                                                                                                           |
| ------ | ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Export | credit (+)          | debit (-) <br>(假设你是Country A的公司) 因为Country B的公司转了Country B的货币在你在Country B的bank Account中 -> 所以相当于你持有了B国家的Assets -> Funds flow out of Country A |
| Import | debit (-)           | credit (+)                                                                                                                                    |
See also: [[Economics (Share N. Gregory Mankiw, Mark P. Taylor).pdf#page=589&selection=44,109,46,70|Economics (Share N. Gregory Mankiw, Mark P. Taylor), page 589]]

> [!example] Example of Paired Transactions
> - A Swiss firm buys a machine for CHF 1000 from a Danish firm
> - The Swiss firm transfers CHF 1000 to the Danish firm’s bank account in Zurich. This transaction creates the following two offsetting entries in the Swiss balance of payments:
>     - It enters the Swiss current account ($NX$) with a negative sign ($ΔNX$ = - CHF 1000) 
>     - It shows up as a ***credit*** in the Swiss financial account ($ΔNCO$ =  - CHF 1000) 
> - That is, Switzerland gets goods in return for assets
> 
> Notes: 这不会导致demand of CHF ⬆️，因为这只代表danish firm持有了瑞士的assets，Denmark‘s capital流入瑞士市场
## Summary

| **Trade Deficit**       | **Balanced Trade**      | **Trade Surplus**       |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Exports < Imports       | Exports = Imports       | Exports > Imports       |
| Net Exports < 0         | Net Exports = 0         | Net Exports > 0         |
| Y < C + I + G           | Y = C + I + G           | Y > C + I + G           |
| Saving < Investment     | Saving = Investment     | Saving > Investment     |
| Net Capital Outflow < 0 | Net Capital Outflow = 0 | Net Capital Outflow > 0 |



# The Prices for International Transactions: Real and Nominal Exchange Rates
## Nominal exchange rate

> nominal exchange rate = 一国货币兑换另一国货币的比率

**它可以用两种写法表达**
- 写法 A：foreign currency per 1 domestic currency
    也就是：1 CHF 能换多少外币
    Example：$1\ \text{CHF} = 1.12\ \text{USD}$
    这时数字越大，表示 1 CHF 更值钱（能买更多外币）。
- 写法 B：domestic currency per 1 foreign currency
    也就是：1 单位外币要多少 CHF 才能换到
    Example：$1\ \text{USD} = 0.89\ \text{CHF}$
        这时数字越小，表示 CHF 更值钱。
        
**这两种写法互为倒数**
$$(1\ \text{CHF in USD}) = \frac{1}{(1\ \text{USD in CHF})}$$
### Appreciation & Depreciation
- ==Appreciation== ([[appreciate]]) refers to an increase in the value of a currency as measured by the amount of foreign currency it can buy
- ==Depreciation== refers to a decrease in the value of a currency as measured by the amount of foreign currency it can buy

> [!example] Example
> - If a Swiss Franc buys more foreign currency, there is an appreciation of the Swiss Franc
> - If it buys less there is a depreciation of the Swiss Franc

## Real Exchange Rates
> The ==real exchange rate== is the rate at which a person can trade the goods and services of one country for the goods and services of another. Example: “瑞士的一篮子商品/一个商品，值多少德国商品/外国商品”

$$
\text{Real exchange rate}
= \frac{\text{Nominal exchange rate (fc/lc)} \times \text{Domestic price}}
{\text{Foreign price}}
$$
- Notes: fc/lc = foreign currency​/local currency
- Lemma: 如果都忽略inflation，那么Nominal和Real变化一致

The real exchange rate compares the prices of domestic goods and **foreign goods in the domestic economy**
> [!example]- Example: beer in Swiss & German 
> If a case of Swiss beer is twice as expensive as German beer, the real exchange rate is 2 cases of German beer per case of Swiss beer.

> [!example]- Example: Big Mac in Swiss & US
> - one good: Big Mac
> - price in the United States P* = $ 6.01
> - price in Switzerland: P = CHF 7.20
> - nominal exchange rate: e = 1.25 $/CHF
> - ***Real exchange rate: ε = 1.25 \* 7.20 / 6.01 = 1.50***
> 
> Meaning: To buy a Swiss Big Mac, someone from the US would have to pay an amount that could buy 1.5 Big Macs in the US
> Overvaluation of the Swiss France relative to the US Dollar: 50%
> Switzerland is expensive with respect to Big Mac
### Appreciation & Depreciation
- A ==depreciation== (fall) in the Swiss real exchange rate means that Swiss goods have become cheaper relative to foreign goods
    - This encourages consumers both at home and abroad to buy more Swiss goods and fewer goods from other countries
    - As a result, Swiss exports rise, and Swiss imports fall, and both of these changes raise Swiss net exports
    i.e. Real exchange rate of Swiss ↓ -> buy more Swiss goods (↑) and fewer goods from other countries (↓) -> X ↑, M ↓
- Conversely, an ==appreciation== in the Swiss real exchange rate means that Swiss goods have become more expensive compared to foreign goods, so Swiss net exports fall
## Purchasing-Power Parity (PPP)
> The ==purchasing-power parity (PPP)== theory is the simplest and most widely accepted theory explaining the variation of currency exchange rates
> ==PPP== is a theory of exchange rates whereby a unit of any given currency should be able to buy the same quantity of goods in all countries 

- ==law of one price== (The PPP theory is based on the principle): 同一种商品，在不同地点（换算成同一种货币后）应该卖同样的价格。
    - 如果不同价，会发生==[[Arbitrage]]==
        如果 A 国更便宜、B 国更贵：
        - 你在 A 国买入（低价市场）
        - 运到 B 国卖出（高价市场）
        - 赚差价
        
        这个过程叫 [[Arbitrage]]，其会带来：
        - 低价市场：Demand ↑ → Price ↑
        - 高价市场：Supply ↑ → Price ↓
        - 最终 两地价格趋同（converge）。
- According to the PPP theory, a currency must have the same purchasing power in all countries and exchange rates move to ensure that
    - ***The nominal exchange rate between two currencies reflects the different price levels in the countries***
    - 如果 PPP 永远成立，真实汇率应当是常数（不怎么波动）。The derivation: [[Economics (Share N. Gregory Mankiw, Mark P. Taylor).pdf#page=595&selection=63,0,65,81|Economics (Share N. Gregory Mankiw, Mark P. Taylor), page 595]]
- Limitations of the PPP theory
    1. Many goods are not easily traded or shipped from one country to another
    2. Tradable goods are not always perfect substitutes when they are produced in different countries

## The Market for Foreign-Currency Exchange
![[Screenshot 2026-01-05 at 02.04.33.png|450]]
- Demand curve: NX
- Supply curve: NCO
### Why does demand curve (NX) slope down?
real exchange rate高（CHF 真实升值）
⇒ 瑞士商品对外国人变贵、外国商品对瑞士人变便宜
⇒ 出口下降、进口上升
⇒ NX 下降
⇒ 外国人为了买瑞士净出口所需要的 CHF 变少
因此：真实汇率越高，对 CHF 的“贸易性需求”（NX）越低，所以需求曲线向下。
*Summary: 本币越贵，净出口越差，所以外国人需要的 CHF 越少。*

### Demand curve (NX) shift
- NX ↑  -> Demand of host country's currency ↑ (Because foreigners need Swiss Francs to buy Swiss net exports)

### The supply curve (NCO) is vertical 
- because the quantity of Swiss Francs supplied is unrelated to the (current) real exchange rate
- It does depend upon the expected change in the real exchange rate during the investment horizon 
    - CHF 的供给**取决于“在投资期内，实际汇率预计会怎么变”**。i.e. 瑞士人是否愿意把 CHF 换成外币、投到国外，  取决于他们**预期未来汇率是升值还是贬值**。
- We (implicitly) assume that the best forecast for the future real exchange rate is its current level
    - This implies that the (expected) change in the real exchange rate equals zero
    
在这个模型里，$NCO$ 的大小先由[[#The Market for Loanable Funds]]决定。一旦决定了本期要净买多少外国资产（$NCO$ 是多少），那就需要拿固定数量的 CHF 去换外币。
⇒ 本期要“卖出多少 CHF 换外币”的数量 = $NCO$（给定）
⇒ 不会因为“当前真实汇率”稍微变动就立刻改掉（短期内当作不变）。
⇒ 所以供给是一条竖直线。

So the simplest reason: Net capital outflow is determined by the real interest rate, not the real exchange rate.

![[Screenshot 2026-01-03 at 02.36.02.png]]In an open economy, government budget deficits
- reduce the supply of loanable funds, 
- drive up the interest rate, 
- crowd out domestic investment, 
- cause net foreign investment (net capital outflow) to fall 
- reduces the supply of Swiss Francs to be exchanged into foreign currency
- Causes the real exchange rate to appreciate
### Trade Policy: an Import Quota

![[Screenshot 2026-01-05 at 02.15.05.png]]
- There is no change in the interest rate because nothing happens in the loanable funds market
- Because foreigners need Swiss Francs to buy Swiss net exports, there is an increased demand for Swiss Francs in the market for foreign-currency
    - This leads to an appreciation of the real exchange rate (⬆️)
- There will be no change in net exports **at long-run**
    - An appreciation of the Swiss Franc in the foreign exchange market encourages imports(⬆️) and discourages exports(⬇️)
    - This [[offset|offsets]] the initial increase in net exports due to the import quota
