# Categories of Unemployment
- Long-run Problem: Natural Rate of Unemployment ^nature-unemployment
    - The natural rate of unemployment is unemployment that does not go away on its own even in the long run 就算经济运行了很久、短期冲击都消退了，仍然“不会自己消失”的那部分失业；也是经济在“正常状态”下通常会经历的失业水平。 
    - ❌ 不是说一个人长期没有就业
    - 而是长期来看，经济中总是有人处在失业状态
    - Monetary policy cannot change the natural rate, but other government policies that strengthen labour markets can (See [[13 Phillips Curve  & Supply-side policies]])
- Short-run Problem: Cyclical Unemployment
    - Cyclical unemployment refers to the year-to-year fluctuations in unemployment around its natural rate
    - It is associated with short-term ups and downs of the business cycle (See [[9 Business Cycles]])


# Definitions and Measurement of Unemployment
1. **Employed** − A person with a job is considered as employed.
2. **Unemployed** − A person is considered as unemployed, if that person does not have a job and(!) is able and available (willing) to work at current wage rates.
    - i.e. `unemployed = !job && able && available_willing`
3. **Not in the Labour Force** − People who do not have a job and are either not able or not available to work
    - i.e. `not_in_labour_force = !job && (!able || !available_willing)`
4. The **Labour Force** is the total number of (employed and unemployed) workers: 
    $$\text{Labour Force} = \text{Employed} + \text{Unemployed}$$

## Labour force participation rate
$$\text{Labour Force Participation Rate} = \frac{\text{Number of persons in the labour force}}{\text{Adult population}} \times 100$$
- Where any person aged ***16 or older*** counts to the adult population (when using the standardized definition)
## Unemployment rate

$$
\text{Unemployment rate} = \frac{\text{Number of unemployed}}{\text{Labour force}} \times 100
$$

## Hysteresis (How Long Are the Unemployed without Work?)
***Hysteresis***(滞后效应) refers to the effect is that the longer people are without work <u>the less likely they are to be hired by firms</u>
# Reasons for Unemployment
In an ideal labour market, wages would adjust to balance the supply and demand for labour, ensuring that all workers would be fully employed
![[Screenshot 2025-12-27 at 02.50.09.png | 400]]
In equilibrium ($W_E$, $Q_E$) there is no unemployment, because:
- Who is willing to work at the given market  = wage is able to do so (market clears)
- Who is not working = does not want to and will not look for work at current wage levels

Two different types of unemployment are responsible for **[[#^nature-unemployment|Nature Unemployment]] (i.e. long-run)**:
## Frictional unemployment
- Unemployment that results from the time that it takes to match workers with jobs that are best suit their tastes and skills
- This type is often thought to explain relatively short spells of unemployment
### due to Job Search
- Job search is the process by which workers find appropriate jobs given their tastes and skills.
- It results from the fact that it takes time for qualified individuals to be matched with appropriate jobs 
- This unemployment is different from the other types of unemployment: 
    - ❌ It is not caused by a wage rate higher than equilibrium (as in the case of structural unemployment) 
    - ✅ It is caused by the time spent searching for the “right” job
- Job search related frictional unemployment is inevitable because the economy changes continuously
#### Sectoral shift / Sectoral unemployment
> Technological and changes in preferences lead to sectoral shifts. 因为经济里**不同行业/地区的需求结构发生变化（sectoral shifts）**，导致某些行业在裁员、另一些行业在招人；但工人从“萎缩部门”转到“扩张部门”需要时间（找工作、搬家、再培训），于是中间出现一段失业。

If the oil price falls, oil extracting firms cut back their production and employment. At the same time, stimulated car sales cause car manufacturers to increase their production and employment

#### Public Policy affect Job Search Time
> Government programs can affect the time it takes unemployed workers to find new jobs and firms to find the right people

- ***Government-run employment agencies*** give out information about job vacancies in order to match workers and jobs more quickly 
- ***Public training programs*** aim to ease the transition of workers from declining to growing industries and to help disadvantaged groups escape poverty.
- ***Unemployment insurance*** is a government program that partially protects workers’ incomes when they become unemployed.
There are advocates and critics of these policies: 
- 👍 Advocates believe the economy to operate more efficiently by keeping labour force more fully employed and by reducing the economy’s [[inherent]] inequities 
- 👎 Critics question government intervention on grounds of efficiency and condemn that it may shrink the incentives to become re-employed 会降低找工作的紧迫性

## Structural unemployment
- Unemployment that results because the number of jobs available in some labour markets is insufficient to provide a job for everyone who wants one (labour supply exceeds labour demand) 
- It is often thought to explain longer spells of unemployment.

### due to Minimum-Wage Laws
![[Minimum-Wage-Laws.png | 400]]

- When the minimum wage is set above the level that balances supply and demand (equilibrium level), it creates unemployment
- Social welfare benefits function like a minimum wage
    - 如果不工作也能拿到较高福利, 那么：
        - 工人不愿意接受低于某个水平的工资
        - 这个“最低可接受工资” = 隐含最低工资（[[reservation wage]]）
### Unions and [[Collective Bargaining]]
- **Insider-Outsider-Problem**: Workers in unions (insiders) reap the benefits of collective bargaining, while workers not in the union (outsiders) bear some of the costs, e.g. unemployment.
    - Empirical Evidence: Union workers earn 10 to 20 percent more than non-union workers
#### Are Unions Good or Bad for the Economy as a Whole?
> No consensus: Union’s influence may be beneficial in some circumstances and adverse in others
### The Theory of Efficiency Wages
Efficiency wages are above-equilibrium wages paid by firms in order to increase worker productivity. The theory of efficiency wages states that firms operate more efficiently if wages are above the equilibrium because of:
- worker health, 
- worker [[turnover]], 
- worker quality,  
- worker effort.
